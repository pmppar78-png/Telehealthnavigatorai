Sleep Issues & Telehealth

Published January 2026 · Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Quality sleep is fundamental to physical and mental health, yet millions struggle with insomnia and other sleep disorders. Telehealth has made evidence-based sleep treatment more accessible than ever. This guide explores online options for addressing sleep problems and improving rest.

Understanding Sleep Disorders

Sleep problems range from occasional difficulty sleeping to chronic disorders requiring medical intervention. Common conditions include:

  • Insomnia: Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking too early
  • Sleep Apnea: Breathing interruptions during sleep (requires medical evaluation)
  • Restless Legs Syndrome: Uncomfortable sensations causing urge to move legs
  • Circadian Rhythm Disorders: Misalignment between sleep patterns and daily schedule
  • Narcolepsy: Excessive daytime sleepiness and sudden sleep episodes
  • Parasomnias: Abnormal behaviors during sleep like sleepwalking or night terrors

Only licensed healthcare professionals can diagnose sleep disorders. Some conditions require in-person sleep studies for accurate diagnosis.

How Telehealth Can Help with Sleep

Online treatment options have proven effective for many sleep issues, particularly insomnia:

  • Convenient access: Work with sleep specialists from home
  • Evidence-based treatment: CBT-I delivered via telehealth matches in-person effectiveness
  • App-based programs: Structured self-guided treatment available anytime
  • Ongoing support: Regular check-ins and adjustment of treatment plans
  • Sleep tracking: Digital tools to monitor progress and patterns

Evidence-Based Sleep Treatments

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)

The gold-standard treatment for chronic insomnia, recommended as first-line therapy before sleep medications. CBT-I typically includes:

  • Sleep restriction: Limiting time in bed to match actual sleep time
  • Stimulus control: Strengthening the association between bed and sleep
  • Cognitive restructuring: Addressing thoughts and worries about sleep
  • Sleep hygiene education: Optimizing habits and environment
  • Relaxation techniques: Reducing physical and mental tension

Medication Management

While CBT-I is preferred, some people benefit from short-term medication. Online psychiatrists can evaluate and prescribe appropriate sleep medications when needed.

Relaxation and Mindfulness

Meditation and relaxation practices can improve sleep quality and reduce the time it takes to fall asleep.

Online Sleep Treatment Options

Digital CBT-I Programs

  • Somryst - FDA-cleared prescription digital therapeutic for chronic insomnia
  • Sleepio - evidence-based CBT-I program with virtual sleep coach
  • CBT-I Coach - free app from the VA (available to everyone)

Sleep and Relaxation Apps

  • Calm - sleep stories, meditations, and relaxation music designed for better sleep
  • Headspace - guided sleep meditations, sleepcasts, and wind-down exercises
  • Sleep Cycle - smart alarm and sleep tracking
  • Pzizz - psychoacoustic mixes for falling asleep

Telehealth Platforms with Sleep Specialists

  • BetterHelp - therapists offering CBT-I and sleep-focused treatment
  • Talkspace - licensed therapists for insomnia and sleep anxiety
  • Cerebral - psychiatry for sleep issues related to anxiety or depression
  • Headway - find in-network sleep specialists

Online Sleep Medicine

  • Sleep medicine physicians via telehealth can evaluate complex sleep issues
  • Home sleep testing is now available for suspected sleep apnea
  • CPAP follow-up can often be done remotely with connected devices

What to Expect in Online Sleep Treatment

  1. Assessment: Review of sleep patterns, habits, medical history, and symptoms
  2. Sleep diary: Tracking sleep times, quality, and daytime functioning
  3. Treatment plan: Personalized approach based on your specific issues
  4. Weekly sessions: Typically 6-8 weeks of structured CBT-I
  5. Behavior changes: Implementing sleep restriction and stimulus control
  6. Progress monitoring: Adjusting treatment based on results

When to Seek Medical Evaluation

Consult a healthcare provider if you experience:

  • Loud snoring or gasping during sleep (possible sleep apnea)
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate sleep time
  • Unusual behaviors during sleep
  • Severe insomnia not improving with behavioral approaches
  • Sleep problems significantly affecting daily functioning
  • Symptoms suggesting underlying medical conditions

Note: Sleep apnea and some other sleep disorders require in-person evaluation and sleep studies for proper diagnosis and treatment.

Sleep Hygiene Tips

While not a substitute for professional treatment, good sleep habits support better rest:

  • Maintain consistent sleep and wake times, even on weekends
  • Create a dark, quiet, cool sleeping environment
  • Limit screen exposure in the hour before bed
  • Avoid caffeine in the afternoon and evening
  • Exercise regularly, but not too close to bedtime
  • Limit alcohol, which disrupts sleep quality
  • Reserve the bed for sleep and intimacy only
  • If you can't sleep, get up rather than lying awake

Sleep and Mental Health

Sleep problems often co-occur with mental health conditions:

  • Anxiety: Racing thoughts can make falling asleep difficult
  • Depression: Can cause insomnia or excessive sleeping
  • PTSD: Nightmares and hypervigilance disrupt sleep
  • Bipolar disorder: Sleep changes often signal mood episodes

Treating underlying mental health conditions often improves sleep, and improving sleep can benefit mental health.
