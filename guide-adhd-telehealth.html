ADHD Diagnosis & Treatment via Telehealth

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) affects millions of adults and children. Telehealth has made ADHD evaluation and treatment more accessible, though it's important to understand the options and limitations of online care.

Understanding ADHD

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting attention, impulse control, and sometimes hyperactivity. Presentations include:

  • Predominantly Inattentive: Difficulty focusing, organizing, following through on tasks
  • Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive: Restlessness, impulsivity, difficulty waiting
  • Combined Presentation: Both inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive symptoms

ADHD can only be diagnosed by qualified healthcare professionals through comprehensive evaluation.

Online ADHD Evaluation

Telehealth ADHD assessments typically include:

  • Detailed symptom questionnaires
  • Clinical interview via video
  • Review of developmental history
  • Assessment of current functioning
  • Rule-out of other conditions with similar symptoms

Limitations of Online Assessment

  • Some cases may require in-person neuropsychological testing
  • Complex cases with multiple conditions may need comprehensive evaluation
  • State regulations may affect what can be prescribed via telehealth

ADHD Treatment Options via Telehealth

Medication Management

Stimulant and non-stimulant medications can be prescribed through telehealth in many states:

  • Stimulant medications: Commonly prescribed but have specific regulations
  • Non-stimulant medications: May have fewer prescribing restrictions
  • Ongoing monitoring: Regular check-ins to assess effectiveness and side effects

Important: ADHD medication prescribing via telehealth is subject to federal and state regulations that vary and may change. Always verify current rules with your provider.

ADHD Coaching and Therapy

Non-medication approaches available online:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Addresses thought patterns and behaviors
  • ADHD Coaching: Practical strategies for organization and time management
  • Skills training: Executive function development
  • Support groups: Connection with others who have ADHD

What to Expect from Online ADHD Care

  1. Initial screening: Questionnaires about symptoms and history
  2. Evaluation appointment: Video consultation with a provider
  3. Diagnosis discussion: If ADHD is identified, treatment options are discussed
  4. Treatment initiation: Starting medication and/or therapy
  5. Follow-up appointments: Regular check-ins to monitor progress
  6. Ongoing management: Adjustments as needed

Considerations for Telehealth ADHD Care

  • State regulations: Rules for prescribing controlled substances vary
  • Medication shortages: Some ADHD medications may have supply issues
  • Pharmacy coordination: Ensure your pharmacy can fill prescriptions
  • Insurance coverage: Verify telehealth psychiatry is covered
  • Documentation: Keep records of your diagnosis and treatment for future providers

ADHD in Different Populations

  • Adults: Often diagnosed later in life; may need different approaches than children
  • Women: ADHD may present differently; often underdiagnosed
  • College students: See Online Therapy for College Students
  • Teens: See Best Online Therapy for Teens
  • Children: Telehealth options may be more limited; see Telehealth for Children
