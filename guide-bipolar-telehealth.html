Bipolar Disorder Telehealth Options

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Bipolar disorder requires ongoing management, often involving both medication and therapy. Telehealth can make accessing consistent care more convenient, though it's important to have appropriate support systems in place.

Understanding Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder involves episodes of mania or hypomania and depression. Types include:

  • Bipolar I: Includes manic episodes
  • Bipolar II: Includes hypomanic and depressive episodes
  • Cyclothymic Disorder: Chronic fluctuating moods

Only licensed healthcare professionals can diagnose bipolar disorder through comprehensive evaluation.

Telehealth for Bipolar Management

Online Psychiatry

Medication is typically central to bipolar treatment. Online psychiatrists can:

  • Evaluate and diagnose (in some cases)
  • Prescribe mood stabilizers and other medications
  • Monitor medication effectiveness and side effects
  • Adjust treatment as needed

Platforms offering psychiatry services include Talkiatry, Cerebral, and Minded.

Therapy

Therapy complements medication for bipolar disorder:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: Managing mood symptoms and negative thoughts
  • Interpersonal and Social Rhythm Therapy: Stabilizing daily routines
  • Family-focused therapy: Improving family communication and support
  • Psychoeducation: Understanding the condition

Considerations for Telehealth with Bipolar

  • Have a crisis plan in place for manic or severe depressive episodes
  • Maintain regular appointments even when feeling well
  • Keep communication open with your provider about symptoms
  • Consider having a trusted person who can help if symptoms escalate
  • Know when to seek in-person or emergency care

When Telehealth May Not Be Sufficient

  • During acute manic or psychotic episodes
  • When hospitalization may be needed
  • If you're newly diagnosed and need comprehensive evaluation
  • During severe depressive episodes with safety concerns
