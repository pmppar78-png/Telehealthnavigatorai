Understanding Mood Disorders

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Mood disorders are mental health conditions that primarily affect your emotional state—your overall feeling of happiness, sadness, or the ability to experience emotions. They're among the most common mental health conditions and are highly treatable. This guide provides an overview of different mood disorders and telehealth options for support.

What Are Mood Disorders?

Mood disorders (also called affective disorders) are characterized by disturbances in a person's emotional state that significantly impact daily life. Everyone experiences mood fluctuations, but mood disorders involve persistent patterns that cause distress and interfere with functioning.

Types of Mood Disorders

Depressive Disorders

  • Major Depressive Disorder: Persistent sadness, loss of interest, and other symptoms lasting at least two weeks
  • Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia): Chronic, milder depression lasting two years or more
  • Postpartum Depression: Depression occurring during pregnancy or after childbirth
  • Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD): Depression related to seasonal changes, typically winter
  • Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder: Severe mood symptoms before menstruation

Bipolar and Related Disorders

  • Bipolar I Disorder: Includes manic episodes (may also include depressive episodes)
  • Bipolar II Disorder: Includes hypomanic episodes and major depressive episodes
  • Cyclothymic Disorder: Chronic fluctuating mood with hypomanic and depressive symptoms

Other Mood Conditions

  • Disruptive Mood Dysregulation Disorder: Severe irritability in children
  • Substance-Induced Mood Disorder: Mood symptoms caused by substances or medications
  • Mood Disorder Due to Medical Condition: Mood symptoms caused by physical illness

Recognizing Mood Disorder Symptoms

Depression Symptoms

  • Persistent sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep disturbances (insomnia or sleeping too much)
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Thoughts of death or suicide

Mania/Hypomania Symptoms

  • Elevated, expansive, or irritable mood
  • Increased energy and activity
  • Decreased need for sleep
  • Racing thoughts or rapid speech
  • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
  • Increased goal-directed activity
  • Impulsive or risky behavior
  • Distractibility

Only licensed healthcare professionals can diagnose mood disorders. If these symptoms sound familiar, consider reaching out for professional evaluation.

Treatment Approaches

Mood disorders are highly treatable. Most people find relief through a combination of approaches:

Psychotherapy

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Identifies and changes negative thought patterns
  • Interpersonal Therapy (IPT): Focuses on relationships and communication
  • Behavioral Activation: Re-engaging with meaningful activities
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): Emotion regulation skills
  • Psychodynamic Therapy: Exploring underlying patterns and past experiences

Medication

  • Antidepressants: SSRIs, SNRIs, and other classes for depression
  • Mood Stabilizers: For bipolar disorder (lithium, valproate, etc.)
  • Atypical Antipsychotics: Sometimes used for mood stabilization
  • Combination approaches: Often most effective when combined with therapy

Lifestyle Approaches

  • Regular exercise
  • Consistent sleep schedule
  • Social connection
  • Stress management
  • Limiting alcohol and substances
  • Light therapy (especially for SAD)

Telehealth for Mood Disorders

Research supports telehealth as effective for mood disorder treatment:

Benefits

  • Accessibility: Connect with care when leaving home feels difficult
  • Consistency: Maintain appointments more easily
  • Comfort: Engage from a familiar environment
  • Options: Access specialists who may not be available locally
  • Flexibility: Schedule around work and life

Online Therapy Platforms

  • BetterHelp - licensed therapists for depression and mood concerns
  • Talkspace - therapy via messaging and video
  • Calmerry - affordable online therapy
  • Headway - in-network therapists
  • Grow Therapy - insurance-covered care

Online Psychiatry

  • Cerebral - therapy and medication management
  • Brightside - specializes in depression and anxiety
  • Talkiatry - in-network psychiatry services
  • Minded - psychiatric medication management

Mood-Specific Platforms

  • Brightside - specifically designed for depression and anxiety
  • Sanvello - app with CBT-based tools for mood

Self-Help Resources

  • Sanvello - mood tracking and coping tools
  • Woebot - AI-based CBT support
  • Calm - meditation and relaxation
  • Headspace - mindfulness for mental health
  • Daylio - mood tracking app
  • Moodfit - mental health tools

Support Organizations

  • DBSA (Depression and Bipolar Support Alliance): Peer support groups, education, and resources
  • NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness): Education and support programs
  • Mental Health America: Screening tools and resources

Getting the Right Diagnosis

Accurate diagnosis is important because treatments differ by condition:

  • Bipolar disorder requires different medication than depression alone
  • Antidepressants alone can trigger mania in bipolar disorder
  • Medical conditions should be ruled out
  • Co-occurring conditions (anxiety, ADHD, etc.) affect treatment

Be thorough in sharing your history with providers, including any past episodes of elevated mood or energy.

When to Seek Immediate Help

Get help right away if:

  • You're having thoughts of suicide or self-harm
  • You're experiencing severe symptoms that interfere with basic functioning
  • You're in a manic episode with risky behavior
  • You're having psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)

Crisis Resources: If you're in crisis, contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988), the Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741), or go to your nearest emergency room.

Recovery and Hope

Mood disorders are among the most treatable mental health conditions:

  • Most people experience significant improvement with treatment
  • Finding the right treatment may take time, but persistence pays off
  • You can live a full, meaningful life with a mood disorder
  • Support from others who understand can be invaluable
  • Learning to manage your condition gets easier over time
