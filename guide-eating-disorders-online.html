Eating Disorder Treatment Online

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Eating disorders are serious mental health conditions that require specialized care. Telehealth has expanded access to evidence-based treatment, making it possible to connect with eating disorder specialists regardless of location. This guide explores online treatment options, what to expect, and how to find appropriate support.

Understanding Eating Disorders

Eating disorders involve complex relationships with food, body image, and eating behaviors. They affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds. Common types include:

  • Anorexia Nervosa: Severe restriction of food intake, intense fear of weight gain, and distorted body image
  • Bulimia Nervosa: Cycles of binge eating followed by compensatory behaviors like purging
  • Binge Eating Disorder: Recurring episodes of eating large amounts with feelings of loss of control
  • Avoidant/Restrictive Food Intake Disorder (ARFID): Extremely limited food preferences not related to body image concerns
  • Other Specified Feeding or Eating Disorder (OSFED): Clinically significant eating disorder symptoms that don't meet full criteria for other diagnoses

Only licensed healthcare professionals can diagnose eating disorders. Early intervention improves outcomes significantly.

How Online Treatment Can Help

Research supports the effectiveness of telehealth for eating disorder treatment, particularly for:

  • Accessibility: Connect with eating disorder specialists who may not be available locally
  • Consistency: Maintain treatment during travel, transitions, or schedule challenges
  • Comfort: Discuss sensitive topics from a private, familiar environment
  • Support: Access to between-session check-ins and meal support
  • Team coordination: Easier communication among treatment team members

Evidence-Based Treatment Approaches

Family-Based Treatment (FBT)

The leading treatment for adolescents with eating disorders, FBT involves parents as active participants in their child's recovery. Virtual FBT has shown effectiveness comparable to in-person treatment.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Eating Disorders (CBT-E)

A specialized form of CBT that addresses the thoughts, feelings, and behaviors maintaining the eating disorder.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Helps develop skills for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and interpersonal effectiveness.

Nutrition Counseling

Working with registered dietitians who specialize in eating disorders to develop healthy relationships with food.

Medical Monitoring

Regular check-ins with physicians to monitor physical health throughout recovery.

Specialized Eating Disorder Telehealth Services

Comprehensive Treatment Programs

  • Equip - virtual eating disorder treatment for all ages with a dedicated five-person care team including therapist, dietitian, physician, peer mentor, and family mentor
  • Project HEAL - nonprofit offering treatment access support and virtual programs
  • Recovery Record - app-based support that integrates with treatment providers

General Telehealth Platforms with ED Specialists

  • BetterHelp - filter for therapists specializing in eating disorders
  • Talkspace - access to licensed therapists with eating disorder expertise
  • Headway - find in-network eating disorder specialists
  • Grow Therapy - insurance-covered therapy options

Supportive Resources

  • National Eating Disorders Association (NEDA) - screening tools, treatment finder, and helpline
  • F.E.A.S.T. - support for families of those with eating disorders
  • Eating Disorder Hope - educational resources and recovery support

Levels of Care

Eating disorder treatment exists on a continuum. Online treatment is typically appropriate for:

  • Outpatient: Weekly therapy sessions while living at home
  • Intensive Outpatient (IOP): Several hours of treatment per week via telehealth
  • Virtual Partial Hospitalization: Full-day programs delivered online

Higher levels of care (residential, inpatient) typically require in-person treatment for medical monitoring and structured meal support.

What to Expect in Online Eating Disorder Treatment

  1. Assessment: Comprehensive evaluation of eating behaviors, medical status, and mental health
  2. Treatment team: Often includes therapist, dietitian, and physician working together
  3. Regular sessions: Weekly or more frequent therapy and nutrition appointments
  4. Meal support: Some programs offer virtual meal support during meals
  5. Family involvement: Particularly important for adolescents and young adults
  6. Progress monitoring: Regular assessment of symptoms, behaviors, and medical stability

When Online Treatment May Not Be Appropriate

Seek in-person or higher-level care if:

  • Medical instability requiring close monitoring (vital signs, labs)
  • Severe malnutrition or rapid weight loss
  • Unable to maintain adequate nutrition at home
  • Active suicidal thoughts or self-harm
  • Severe symptoms not responding to outpatient treatment
  • Need for 24-hour supervision and support

Crisis Resources: If you're in crisis, contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988), the NEDA helpline (1-800-931-2237), or go to your nearest emergency room.

Supporting Recovery

  • Commit to working with your full treatment team
  • Be honest about behaviors and symptoms
  • Include trusted family members or support people when appropriate
  • Follow meal plans and nutrition guidance
  • Attend all scheduled appointments
  • Practice skills learned in therapy between sessions
  • Connect with peer support communities
