OCD Treatment Online

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) requires specialized treatment, and telehealth has made evidence-based care more accessible. This guide explores online options for OCD treatment and what to look for in a provider.

Understanding OCD

OCD involves unwanted, intrusive thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions) performed to reduce anxiety. Common themes include:

  • Contamination: Fear of germs, illness, or contamination
  • Harm: Fear of harming oneself or others
  • Symmetry/order: Need for things to be "just right"
  • Unwanted thoughts: Intrusive thoughts about taboo topics
  • Checking: Repeatedly checking locks, appliances, etc.

Only licensed healthcare professionals can diagnose OCD.

Gold-Standard Treatment: ERP

Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) is the most effective treatment for OCD:

  • Gradually facing feared situations (exposure)
  • Resisting compulsive behaviors (response prevention)
  • Learning that anxiety decreases naturally without compulsions
  • Typically 12-20+ sessions with a trained therapist

NOCD specializes specifically in ERP-based OCD treatment delivered via telehealth.

Finding the Right OCD Therapist

Important questions to ask:

  • Are you trained specifically in ERP?
  • What percentage of your caseload is OCD?
  • How do you structure ERP treatment?
  • Do you do exposures during sessions or only assign homework?

Not all therapists are trained in ERP. Seeking a specialist is important for effective treatment.
