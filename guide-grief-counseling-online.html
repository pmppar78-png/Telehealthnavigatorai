Grief Counseling Online

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Grief is a natural response to loss, but navigating it can be overwhelming. Online grief counseling provides accessible support for those coping with the death of a loved one, relationship endings, or other significant losses. This guide explores telehealth options for grief support and what to expect from online bereavement services.

Understanding Grief

Grief is a deeply personal experience that manifests differently for everyone. Common aspects of grief include:

  • Emotional responses: Sadness, anger, guilt, anxiety, numbness, or relief
  • Physical symptoms: Fatigue, changes in appetite, sleep disturbances, physical aches
  • Cognitive effects: Difficulty concentrating, confusion, preoccupation with the loss
  • Behavioral changes: Social withdrawal, crying, restlessness, searching behaviors
  • Spiritual questions: Questioning beliefs, searching for meaning

Grief does not follow a linear path or predictable timeline. There is no "right" way to grieve.

Types of Loss

Grief can arise from many types of loss, including:

  • Death of a loved one: Spouse, parent, child, friend, or pet
  • Relationship loss: Divorce, breakup, or estrangement
  • Health-related loss: Diagnosis of serious illness, loss of abilities
  • Life transitions: Job loss, retirement, moving, children leaving home
  • Pregnancy and infant loss: Miscarriage, stillbirth, infertility
  • Anticipatory grief: Grieving an expected loss before it occurs
  • Collective loss: Community tragedies, natural disasters, pandemics

How Online Grief Counseling Helps

Virtual grief support offers meaningful benefits:

  • Accessibility: Get support without leaving home during difficult times
  • Comfort: Process difficult emotions in a familiar environment
  • Flexibility: Schedule sessions around caregiving or other responsibilities
  • Privacy: Grieve without concerns about being seen at a counselor's office
  • Continuity: Maintain support during travel or relocation
  • Connection: Access grief specialists who may not be available locally

Approaches to Grief Counseling

Supportive Counseling

Creating a safe space to express feelings, share memories, and process the loss at your own pace.

Cognitive Behavioral Approaches

Addressing unhelpful thought patterns and developing coping strategies for grief-related challenges.

Meaning-Making Approaches

Finding purpose and reconstructing meaning after loss, integrating the loss into your ongoing life story.

Complicated Grief Treatment

Specialized therapy for prolonged grief disorder when grief remains intensely debilitating over time.

EMDR for Traumatic Loss

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing can help process traumatic aspects of sudden or violent losses.

  • Talkspace - licensed therapists experienced in bereavement counseling
  • Calmerry - affordable online therapy for grief support
  • Headway - find in-network grief counselors

What to Expect in Online Grief Counseling

  1. Initial meeting: Sharing your story and what brings you to counseling
  2. Assessment: Understanding your grief experience and support needs
  3. Goal setting: Identifying what you hope to gain from counseling
  4. Regular sessions: Ongoing support as you process your loss
  5. Coping strategies: Developing tools for managing difficult moments
  6. Gradual integration: Finding ways to honor your loss while moving forward

When Grief Needs Professional Help

Consider seeking support if you experience:

  • Grief that feels unbearable or overwhelming
  • Difficulty functioning in daily life after several months
  • Intense longing and preoccupation with the deceased
  • Avoidance of reminders of the loss
  • Feelings of meaninglessness or that life isn't worth living
  • Difficulty accepting the reality of the loss
  • Isolation from friends and family
  • Using substances to cope with grief

If you're having thoughts of suicide: Contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline (call or text 988) or go to your nearest emergency room.

Supporting Yourself Through Grief

  • Allow yourself to feel whatever emotions arise without judgment
  • Maintain basic self-care: sleep, nutrition, gentle movement
  • Accept support from friends and family when offered
  • Be patient with yourself—grief takes time
  • Create rituals or ways to honor your loved one
  • Limit major decisions when possible in early grief
  • Consider joining a grief support group
  • Seek professional help if you're struggling
