PTSD & Trauma Therapy Online

Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) and trauma-related conditions can be effectively treated through telehealth. This guide explores online options for trauma therapy and what to consider when seeking help.

Understanding PTSD and Trauma

Trauma can result from experiencing or witnessing distressing events. PTSD may develop when symptoms persist and significantly impact daily life:

  • Intrusive symptoms: Flashbacks, nightmares, intrusive memories
  • Avoidance: Avoiding reminders of the trauma
  • Negative changes: In thoughts, mood, or beliefs
  • Hyperarousal: Being easily startled, difficulty sleeping, irritability

Evidence-Based Trauma Treatments

Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)

Helps process traumatic memories and change unhelpful beliefs about the trauma. Well-suited to telehealth delivery.

Prolonged Exposure (PE)

Gradually confronting trauma-related memories and situations in a safe, controlled way.

EMDR

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing uses bilateral stimulation while processing traumatic memories. Can be adapted for online delivery.

Trauma-Focused CBT

Cognitive-behavioral approaches specifically designed for trauma.

Is Online Trauma Therapy Right for You?

Online therapy may be appropriate if:

  • You feel safe and stable enough to engage in trauma processing
  • You have a private, secure space for sessions
  • You're not in immediate crisis
  • You have coping skills to manage between sessions

Consider in-person or more intensive treatment if you have severe symptoms, active suicidal thoughts, or difficulty functioning.
