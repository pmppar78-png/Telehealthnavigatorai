Couples Therapy Online

Published January 2026 · Educational information – not medical advice or diagnosis

Every relationship faces challenges, and seeking help is a sign of commitment to your partnership. Online couples therapy has made it easier than ever to access professional support for your relationship. This guide explores virtual options for couples counseling and what to expect from the process.

When to Consider Couples Therapy

Couples therapy can help at any stage of a relationship. Common reasons couples seek help include:

  • Communication problems: Difficulty expressing needs, frequent misunderstandings, or shutdown during conflict
  • Conflict patterns: Recurring arguments that never get resolved
  • Trust issues: Rebuilding after infidelity or addressing jealousy
  • Intimacy concerns: Physical or emotional distance in the relationship
  • Life transitions: Adjusting to parenthood, job changes, or retirement
  • Different values or goals: Disagreements about finances, parenting, or life direction
  • Relationship maintenance: Strengthening a good relationship before problems arise
  • Deciding about the relationship: Considering separation or divorce

Benefits of Online Couples Therapy

Virtual couples counseling offers unique advantages:

  • Convenience: No need to coordinate travel for both partners
  • Scheduling flexibility: Evening and weekend options accommodate busy lives
  • Comfort: Participate from your own home environment
  • Long-distance couples: Join sessions from different locations when apart
  • Privacy: No chance of running into acquaintances at a therapist's office
  • Access: Work with specialists who may not be available locally
  • Childcare: Easier to arrange when you don't need to leave home

Evidence-Based Approaches to Couples Therapy

Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT)

Helps couples identify and change negative interaction patterns by focusing on emotional responses and attachment needs.

Gottman Method

Based on decades of research, focuses on building friendship, managing conflict, and creating shared meaning in relationships.

Cognitive Behavioral Couples Therapy

Addresses thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to relationship problems.

Imago Relationship Therapy

Focuses on understanding how childhood experiences shape relationship patterns and improving communication through structured dialogue.

Discernment Counseling

For couples where one or both partners are considering divorce, helping clarify whether to work on the relationship or separate.

Online Couples Therapy Platforms

Couples-Focused Platforms

  • ReGain - dedicated online platform for couples therapy, offering video sessions with licensed therapists specializing in relationship issues
  • Lasting - app-based couples therapy program with optional counselor support
  • OurRelationship - research-based online program for couples

General Therapy Platforms with Couples Services

  • BetterHelp - offers couples therapy through their platform
  • Talkspace - licensed marriage and family therapists available
  • Headway - find in-network couples therapists
  • Grow Therapy - insurance-covered couples counseling
  • Rula - couples therapy covered by insurance

Relationship Support Apps

  • Paired - daily questions and exercises for couples
  • Relish - relationship coaching and quizzes
  • Love Nudge - based on the Five Love Languages
  • Gottman Card Decks - conversation starters based on research

What to Expect in Online Couples Therapy

  1. Initial assessment: Understanding each partner's perspective and relationship history
  2. Goal setting: Identifying what you want to achieve together
  3. Regular sessions: Typically weekly, with both partners present
  4. Skill building: Learning communication and conflict resolution techniques
  5. Homework: Practicing new skills between sessions
  6. Progress evaluation: Regular check-ins on relationship improvement

Tips for Successful Online Couples Therapy

  • Both partners engaged: Both people need to be willing to participate
  • Private space: Ensure you won't be overheard or interrupted
  • Same room or separate: Discuss with your therapist what works best
  • Technology ready: Test your connection before sessions
  • Be honest: Therapy works best with open communication
  • Do the homework: Practice skills between sessions
  • Give it time: Relationship change takes consistent effort

When Online Couples Therapy May Not Be Appropriate

In-person therapy or other interventions may be needed when:

  • There is domestic violence or abuse in the relationship
  • One partner has active, untreated addiction
  • Either partner is in acute mental health crisis
  • Technology limitations prevent effective participation
  • One partner is unwilling to participate in good faith

If you are in an abusive relationship: Contact the National Domestic Violence Hotline at 1-800-799-7233 or text START to 88788. Individual safety planning may be more appropriate than couples therapy.

Costs and Insurance

Couples therapy costs vary:

  • Online platforms may charge $60-$150+ per session
  • Some platforms offer subscription models with unlimited messaging
  • Insurance coverage for couples therapy varies by plan
  • Some therapists offer sliding scale fees
  • Check if your employer's EAP covers couples sessions

Individual Therapy and Couples Work

Sometimes one or both partners benefit from individual therapy alongside couples work:

  • Processing personal issues that affect the relationship
  • Addressing mental health conditions
  • Working through trauma
  • Developing individual coping skills

Discuss with your couples therapist if individual therapy might be helpful.
