Telehealth for Primary Care: Everything You Need to Know

Published January 2026 · Comprehensive educational resource – not medical advice

Primary care telehealth allows you to consult with doctors for non-emergency health concerns from the comfort of your home. This guide explores how primary care telehealth works, what conditions can be addressed, and how to make the most of virtual doctor visits.

What Is Primary Care Telehealth?

Primary care telehealth provides access to general medical care through virtual appointments. This can include:

  • Urgent care consultations: Same-day care for non-emergency concerns
  • Routine check-ins: Follow-up appointments and wellness visits
  • Prescription management: Refills and medication adjustments
  • Lab result reviews: Discussion of test results
  • Referral coordination: Getting referrals to specialists when needed
  • Health questions: Addressing general health concerns

Platforms like Teladoc, MDLive, PlushCare, and K Health offer primary care telehealth services.

Conditions That Can Be Addressed via Telehealth

Commonly Treated Conditions

  • Respiratory infections: Cold, flu, sinus infections, bronchitis
  • Allergies: Seasonal allergies, allergic reactions
  • Skin conditions: Rashes, acne, eczema (with photos/video)
  • Urinary issues: UTIs, bladder concerns
  • Digestive problems: Nausea, diarrhea, acid reflux
  • Eye infections: Pink eye, styes
  • Minor injuries: Sprains, strains, minor cuts (assessment and guidance)
  • Headaches: Migraines, tension headaches
  • Insomnia: Sleep difficulties

Ongoing Care Management

  • Chronic conditions: Diabetes, hypertension, asthma (monitoring and management)
  • Mental health: Anxiety, depression (see Mental Health Telehealth Guide)
  • Weight management: Through services like Noom
  • Medication management: Reviewing and adjusting ongoing medications

Prescriptions Available via Telehealth

Many medications can be prescribed through telehealth visits:

  • Antibiotics for infections
  • Antihistamines and allergy medications
  • Blood pressure medications
  • Diabetes medications
  • Birth control (through services like Nurx)
  • Asthma inhalers
  • Anti-nausea medications
  • Some mental health medications

Note: Controlled substances have stricter regulations and may require in-person evaluation in some states. Your provider will advise you on what's appropriate for telehealth.

When Telehealth Is NOT Appropriate

Primary care telehealth has limitations. Seek in-person or emergency care for:

  • Medical emergencies: Chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding
  • Severe symptoms: High fever unresponsive to treatment, severe pain
  • Physical examination needs: Conditions requiring hands-on assessment
  • Lab work: Blood draws, urine samples, imaging
  • Procedures: Stitches, injections, biopsies
  • Complex conditions: New or worsening symptoms requiring comprehensive evaluation

Emergency Warning: If you are experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. Telehealth cannot provide emergency care.

How Primary Care Telehealth Works

Before Your Appointment

  1. Choose a service: Select a telehealth platform or use your regular doctor's virtual option
  2. Create an account: Provide personal and insurance information
  3. Describe your concern: Complete intake questions about your symptoms
  4. Schedule or request visit: Book an appointment or request on-demand care
  5. Prepare information: Have your medication list, symptoms, and questions ready
  6. Test your technology: Ensure camera, microphone, and internet work

During Your Appointment

  • Connect with your provider via video or phone
  • Describe your symptoms and concerns
  • Answer questions about your health history
  • Show affected areas if relevant (skin, throat, etc.)
  • Receive assessment and recommendations
  • Get prescriptions sent to your pharmacy if needed

After Your Appointment

  • Review visit summary and instructions
  • Pick up any prescriptions from your pharmacy
  • Schedule follow-up if needed
  • Contact provider if symptoms worsen

Types of Primary Care Telehealth Services

On-Demand Urgent Care

See a doctor quickly for urgent but non-emergency concerns:

  • Teladoc - 24/7 access to doctors
  • MDLive - on-demand virtual urgent care
  • Doctor On Demand - quick access to physicians
  • Amwell - virtual doctor visits

Virtual Primary Care Practices

Ongoing care with a consistent provider:

  • PlushCare - virtual primary care with a dedicated doctor
  • K Health - AI-assisted primary care
  • HealthTap - virtual primary care membership

Cash-Pay Options

Affordable care without insurance:

  • Sesame - transparent cash pricing
  • Many platforms offer uninsured rates

Cost of Primary Care Telehealth

Costs vary based on service type and insurance:

  • With insurance: Typically $0-$75 copay depending on your plan
  • Without insurance: Usually $50-$100+ per visit
  • Subscription services: Monthly fees for unlimited or discounted visits
  • Employer benefits: Many employers offer free or reduced-cost telehealth

Prescription savings through GoodRx can help reduce medication costs after your visit.

Tips for Effective Telehealth Visits

  • Prepare your space: Find a quiet, well-lit, private location
  • Gather information: Have your medication list and relevant health records
  • List your questions: Write down what you want to discuss
  • Describe symptoms clearly: Include when they started, severity, and what makes them better or worse
  • Take photos: If relevant, photograph skin conditions or affected areas before the visit
  • Be honest: Share complete information about your health and lifestyle
  • Take notes: Write down the provider's recommendations
  • Ask for clarification: Don't hesitate to ask questions if something isn't clear

Telehealth and Your Regular Doctor

You can often use telehealth while maintaining care with your existing physician:

  • Many primary care practices now offer their own telehealth options
  • Use urgent care telehealth for after-hours or weekend needs
  • Request that telehealth providers send records to your regular doctor
  • Continue in-person visits for annual physicals and comprehensive care
